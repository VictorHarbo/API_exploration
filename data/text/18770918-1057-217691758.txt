BRISBANE). (from our own correspondent.) September 17. At a meeting of the Board of Health to- day it was decided that the immigrant ship Windsor Castle is not to be quarantined. The Right Rev. Dr. O'Quinn administered the rite of confirmation to over 200 children and adults on Sunday afternoon. At a meeting of the Council of the National Association to-day it was resolved to call on Mr. Low, the Secretary, to resign, and to allow him three months salary as compensation for loss of office. The Council unanimously recommended Mr. Layton for the office. A petition was presented to the Governor to-day against the further imprisonment of tho Danish sailors, who were sentenced to a year's imprisonment for breaking quarantine from the ship Charles Dickens.
