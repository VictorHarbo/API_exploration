The Records Management Systems Working Group is one of a number of working groups examining shared IT systems for the Commonwealth. It was set up under the umbrella of the Office of Government Information Technology and reports to the Shared Systems Steering Committee. It is primarily interested in the identification of a number of records information management systems which would serve the common needs of Commonwealth agencies. It is convened by Vince McMahon, from the Department of Immigration and Ethnic Affairs, and it has membership from over twenty agencies, both large and small. The Working Group has developed a set of Fundamental Evaluation Criteria for Records Management Information Systems and is also using a Generic Fundamental Criteria which relate more broadly to scalability, inter-operability, security, management reporting and the like.
