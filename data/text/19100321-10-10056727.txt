AUSTRALIAN IMMIGRATION; me DANISH GOVERNMENT'S WARNING. RESENTED BY MR. WADE. * SYDNEY, March 20. The Premier (Mr. Deakin) has received from the Agent-General the text of the warning issued by the Danish Government against immigration. "The Minister for Foreign Affairs publishes the following: As inquiries regarding the state of affairs in Australia and New Zealand are addressed to the Minister of Foreign Affairs from day to day, the Ministry points out that as the difficulty for immigrants to subsist in those countries have latterly largely increased, Danes in search of employment are earnestly dissuaded from going to the country named. The Premier has forwarded the cable gram for publication in the British press. ) Mr. Wade,. Premier of New South Wales, strongly resents the publication by the Danish Minister for Foreign Affairs, of the warning against Australia as a field for emigration. He states that no country in the world to-day offers a better field for the industrious worker than New South Wales. Of 10,000 genuine immigrants of the settler class in 1909, all were satisfactorily settled without delay. There are practically no unemployed at the present time, but there are huge unsatisfied de amends for agricultural and domestic labour, and remunerative wages. Since 1906 New South Wales assisted 14,009 Immigrants, the Government guarantee- ing work for agriculturists and domestic servants, and placing them in satisfactory employment on the day of landing. Hundreds of these immigrants have made homes on the land, owning freehold, and are now prosperous farmers, till the others are earning about double the wages received in older countries.
