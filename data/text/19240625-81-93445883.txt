SETTLERS FOR CANADA WINNIPEG.' Monday. Canada stands to, benefit greatly from the qbota restrictions imposed by the United States on immigration, states Mr. Car] Jacobsen, Canadian immigration agent, who has the special duty of placing Scandinavian immigrants on the land. The United States quota from Denmark is already filled. It is placed at 5600 people. For that reason Canada will receive this years 12,000 Danish settlers. Before, the war about *6000 Danish settler? emigrated every year, nearly* all going to the United States. During the war this surplus stopped, and there is now a large number, many thousands, wanting to go to Canada. They are all experienced farmers, and will take up mixed fanning, many of them being expert butter-makers.
