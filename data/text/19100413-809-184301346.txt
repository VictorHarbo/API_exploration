IMMIGRATION. DANES AND AUSTRALIA. LONDON, 11th April. Correspondence has passed between Mr. T. A. Coglilan, Agent-General for New South ' Wales, and M. Sebavenius, the Danisli.Minister for Foreign Affairs, in re gard to the warning issued in February last to intending Danish emigrants to the Commonwealth, who were informed that the difficulties of subsistence in both Aus tralia and New Zealand had increased con siderably of late. M. Sebavenius has explained to Mr. Coghlan that the warning was given on what was considered sufficient authority. Tho Danish consuls in Australia, the Min ister stated, would he instructed to report on the question, and if the statements were found to be untrue or exaggerated - the Danish Foreign Office would issue the neces sary correction.
