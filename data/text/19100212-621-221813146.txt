AUSTRALIA. The "Daily Chronicle" says that the decision of the Victorian Government to despatch Mr Elwood Mead abroad to find settlers for the irrigation districts represents a complete reversal of the immigration policy of Victoria, which, from being the most backward, be comes the most enterprising State of the Commonwealth. The first of the torpedo boat destroy ers being constructed on the Clyde for the Commonwealth of Australia has been christened the Parramatta by Mrs Asquith, wife of the Prime Minister. Count Marner, Consul-General in Aus tralia for Sweden, has issued a warning in the Swedish newspapers against his countrymen emigrating to Australia. Following this the Danish Foreign Min istry has issued a similar warning in the local newspapers. Intending immi grants are told that the difficulties of subsistence in both the Commonwealth and Dominion have increased consider ably of late.
